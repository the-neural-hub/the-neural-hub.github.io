APPLICATION OF MACHINE LEARNING IN YOUTUBE

DATE : 25/08/2021

INTRODUCTION

This article discusses about another tech giant, a leading social media platform with more than two billion daily users. YouTube it is. A popular social media platform for sharing and streaming videos not restricted to any topic and having over 2 billion users, running successfully since 2005. It all stated with the first YouTube video "me at the zoo”. The main reason YouTube is still successful and attracting more users everyday is due to the reason of right audience attraction and right recommendations that makes it a platform with one billion hours of video streaming and sharing everyday.





CONTENTS

Just like it’s parent company Google, YouTube also uses ML based algorithms and aims to provide the users with relavant,personalized videos. To brief in layman terms, the algorithm knows what you already watched and what others similar to your liking have watched and gives new recommendations based on this data.


YouTube has multiple sections to interest the user such as the Home, Search, Recommended Videos, Trending, and Subscriptions.The YouTube algorithm works in all these areas. The algorithm suggests the right video in the feed based on the previous history. This algorithm is clearly visible when the user is having the watch history turned off vs when it is on. There are clearly better suggestions based on the interest, popularity and the region the user is from. Similar to the way that Google no longer accepts keyword stuffing, YouTube wants to see videos that are created for your audience instead of trying to beat the system.


HOME AND RECOMMENDED RESULTS ALGORITHM

The first part of the algorithm for home and recommended videos focuses solely on the users’ history. All of the below questions help YouTube select videos that are custom chosen for the satisfaction of the user.

  • What have they watched before?
  • Do they mostly listen to music?
  • Do they watch vlogs?
  • What channels do they frequently visit?

There are also options present in each suggestion in the feed to tell if you’re not interested in that video. You can simply click on NOT INTERESTED to not let the algorithm suggest such videos in the future.

Secondly, the algorithm takes into account the interactions between top videos and users with similar interests. YouTube, for example, is likely to have a group of users who are regularly listening to the same artists and bands. Users will then be able to view the top videos viewed by this similar audience and those videos will be ranked high for these users. It won't suggest the same videos and channels over and over again. As the algorithm understands that users are interested in many different things, it will suggest videos based upon everything they have previously watched.


TRENDING RESULTS ALGORITHM

Trending videos are compiled using an algorithm that pulls down videos that are new and popular within a particular country. Users cannot pay to have their video shown in trending results since trending videos are never sponsored. Among the top trending videos are some that people expect, such as new songs, movie trailers but others can vary based on factors such as the rate of growth in views and the source of those views.


SEARCH RESULTS ALGORITHM

In terms of search, video relevance is mostly determined by the relevant keyword phrases in the title and description, as well as the total duration of the video. It is obvious that the algorithm wants to make sure that the keyword used by you in your search is in the video's title and/or description when it delivers the video results. Furthermore, the algorithm also considers whether a user has ever watched a video on a given channel before.


SUBSCRIPTION RESULTS ALGORITHM

YouTube uses view velocity to rank subscribed channel results, which is a number that quantifies how many subscribers watch a channel video right after it has been published. Videos will be ranked higher depending on how high 'view velocity' is. When determining an order of videos, YouTube takes into account a channel's number of subscribers!


Not only does the algorithm help the viewers to get the right video to spend their leisure time, but also helps the content creators to make their video rank higher among others with some tips that follow.


Tip to get featured in “Home” page - You should strive to maintain user engagement as long as possible. No matter how long or short your videos are, keeping your audience interested and engaged should be your main objective.

Tip to rank higher in the “Trending” section - For your videos to appear in Trending, you should produce content that is broadly relatable, appealing, and easy to share.

Tip to rank higher in the “Search” results - For your videos, make sure the description contains the keyword in the extended description, but don't keyword stuff or write irrelevant content.

Tip to improve in the “Subscription” results - Be genuine when you ask for subscribers. Your subscribers will be much more likely to watch your channel if you explain why subscribing to your channel is beneficial for them.



CONCLUSION

With the very existence of Youtube through website, mobile applications and network television, it has helped and enabled the society to more directly engage with Youtube creators such as TEDTalks, celebrities and even assists in promoting politics. Along with the above mentioned algorithms, there are few beneficial applications that Youtube heavily focus on such as fake news, "Up Next" feature and etc. Thus, the Artifical Intelligence plays a pivotal role in the Google's online video sharing platform.
