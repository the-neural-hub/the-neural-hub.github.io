APPLICATION OF MACHINE LEARNING IN INSTAGRAM

DATE : 15/07/2021

INTRODUCTION

The one reason for the huge success of Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence is the proper and effective use of the abundant valuable resource, the USER DATA. Before 2010, nobody even cared about Machine Learning or what neural networks were. But once we started obtaining great predictions and classification by the right use of data in the machine learning applications, now every industry is feeding it's data to such models to obtain better predictions and improve their business by making it more user-centric instead of being business centric.


There are so many domains and use cases that use ML and AI today. Every industry makes use of it's data and improves the user experience by providing them with more specific solutions for their problems. Let it be an image search algorithm or auto complete algorithm or job search, the list goes on.


Now we’ll discuss one such use case that made use of AI and ML to earn billions. The best in the social media industry. Yes, we are talking about Instagram. We’ll look into how Instagram makes use of the user data to provide better experience while using the application. Everything we do with the application is useful data to learn something about the user. Not only in Instagram, even in webpages the clicks we do, the things we select, each define our preferences and add up in the bucket of the user data.



CONTENTS

How does Instagram use your data?


INTEREST

We usually see only contents that we like and that we usually want to see first. And there is a specific ordering of the posts we see in the feed. Who actually determines this feed order? Pretty straight forward. Some algorithm that runs in the back end server. But how is the order of the photos and videos determined? You’d have guessed it by now. Yes, it is based on the likelihood of the post that you want to see first which is calculated based on the past data present in the system. The data which includes what your interests are,what pages you follow and visit frequently. The posts you’ve liked recently, the hashtags you follow or the hashtags that occur repeatedly in the posts you like and what genre they belong to (e.g. travel, food, fashion, sports, etc). Consider you follow 200 pages, but you regularly interact with only 30-40 pages. So Instagram prefers to show those pages' posts to you with higher priority than the remaining 160-170 pages' content.

TIMELINESS

At the same time, even if you’re inactive for sometime and back, it doesn’t suggest posts from the past right? And it shouldn’t. Hence it shows only the recent posts that are favoured by you and you want to see first i.e. more relevant. This implies that recent posts likely rank higher in your feed and that the timing of your post is still relevant. Instagram algorithm re-orders only the new posts between your current visit and your last visit. The posts you visited the last time are not considered for the next round of feed display order and hence not shown again, obviously.

RELATIONSHIP

And no matter how many accounts you follow, you should see your best friend’s latest posts. Instagram algorithm studies your past interactions to determine your “best friends”. Instagram determines the people you care about by different interactions you do within the application.

  • People whose content you like (possibly including stories and live videos)
  • People you direct message
  • People you search for
  • People you know in real life
When you search (regularly) for certain profiles, it likely indicates that you are interested in the account’s posts and might not have seen them on your feed. Instagram might then rank their posts higher on your feed so that you don’t have to search for their profiles to see their posts, improving your Instagram experience.

There are also additional criteria that play a smaller part in your Instagram feed rankings.

FREQUENCY

Every time a user opens up Instagram, the algorithm will try to show the best posts since their last visit. So if you open Instagram once daily, you’ll likely see the posts that Instagram’s algorithm feels are the most relevant for that day.

However, if you use insta often, you probably won't miss out on anything, right?

USAGE

If a user prefers short visits to Instagram, the algorithm will ensure it shows the most relevant posts first, whereas for users who prefer longer browsing sessions it may provide a deeper catalog of fresh content to browse.

EXPLORE TAB

An interesting feature of instagram is the explore tab, that shows varities of content. Basically Instagram recognizes accounts that are more or less similar to one another by adopting a technique termed as “embedding”.

So to make recommendations, it starts by observing the “seed accounts” which are the accounts the users have interacted with in the past.

It then discovers the accounts that are similar to these and selects 500 pieces of content from them.

These content pieces are then filtered and then the the top 25 posts are ranked on the basis of how probable a user is to interact with each one, sent to the first page of the user’s explore tab.

DEALING WITH SPAM

With an abundance of content being shared every day across the app, some of it is bound to be spam.

Now how are these spam messages detected?

Instagram makes use of Artificial Intelligence’s text analytics algorithm for dealing with spam messages.

Its spam filter can detect spam messages in over 9 languages that include English, Arabic, and Chinese. Once detected, these messages are automatically removed.

CYBER BULLYING?

Instagram is considered to be the biggest cyberbullying social media site and they became the first to use Machine Learning to automatically remove offensive posts.

Based on the text analytics algorithm it identifies spam and removes it. It is a solution to identify and eliminate comments that violate Instagram’s Community Guidelines.



CONCLUSION

This makes clear how the time spent on Instagram, makes more profit for the business by utilizing it properly using modern technologies of Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning and Text Analytics.
