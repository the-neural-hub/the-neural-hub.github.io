APPLICATION OF MACHINE LEARNING IN NETFLIX

DATE : 20/08/2021

INTRODUCTION

In the previous post about Instagram, we’ve seen how effectively the user data is used to curate the feed for each profile based on the personal interests and likings. As mentioned in the previous post, this post is also related to another use case of Machine Learning and AI in the real time industry. This is another successful organisation, to be more specific it is a digital streaming company winning in the entertainment industry among its competitors. YES! We are going to see about the use of data in Netflix, one of the giants in the entertainment industry.


NETFLIX earned its reputation as one of media's most successful brands through maintaining a good balance between automation and human interaction. What truly makes Netflix stand out is its unmatched recommendation system, which utilizes AI capabilities to improve the brand’s product offering and the user experience. We also know that the recommendation system was solely responsible for Netflix’s success in its initial days. By providing personalized content recommendations, it became very user friendly and encouraged the user to continue in this platform even when new companies emerged.


First started out as a DVD rental service, the American digital streaming service has now expanded to an array of digital content. With more than 208 million subscribers around the world, it is currently the largest streaming service.

PERSONALIZATION OF MOVIE RECOMMENDATIONS

The principle behind the working of recommendation system is Users who watch X are most likely to watch Y. This is the most well known feature of Netflix. The X and Y are nothing but the shows or movies streamed on the platform.

In order to keep you on the platform, Netflix uses the watch history of other users with similar tastes to offer recommendations for what you may be most interested in viewing next. Which means if you are watching a sequence of funny series on Netflix, it uses the data of other users who have the similar viewing history and uses that to recommend the next set of shows for you.


But the recommendation algorithm does not stop with this, the best is coming next.


AUTO-GENERATION AND PERSONALIZATION OF THUMBNAILS / ARTWORK

Using the movie’s original art as the only thumbnail for every single person most likely won’t yield the highest click through rates. Every person viewing the same poster isn’t going to watch the movie for sure. But if the movie has some part of the story, which resembles the user’s liking, that can be used in the movie poster to lure the user into watching that particular movie/series. Here comes the role of personalized movie posters/ thumbnails.


HOW TO CREATE A DIFFERENT THUMBNAIL FOR EACH USER THAT IS OPTIMIZED TO INCREASE CLICK RATES?

Using thousands of video frames from an existing movie or show as a starting point , Netflix adds tags to these images then ranks each image in an effort to identify which thumbnails have the highest likelihood of resulting in your click. It is calculated by looking at what other people, who are similar to you, have clicked on. It is possible that users favoring certain actors or movie genres are more likely to click thumbnails with those actors or image attributes.


HOW DOES NETFLIX CREATE THESE PERSONALIZED THUMBNAILS / ARTWORK?

A single episode with a running duration of one hour has more than 86,000 static video frames in it. These video frames can each individually be assigned certain tags that are used to filter down to the best thumbnail candidates through a set of tools and algorithms called Aesthetic Visual Analysis (AVA). This is designed to find the best custom thumbnail image out of every static frame of the video.

  • Netflix Annotation — Netflix creates metadata for each frame including brightness (.67), # of faces (3) , skin tones (.2), probability of nudity (.03), level of motion blur (4), symmetry (.4)
  • Netflix Image Ranking — Netflix now picks out specific images that are highest and most clickable based on the user profile


PRODUCT CONSIDERATIONS IN PERSONALIZED IMAGE THUMBNAILS

Using the clicking behavior of other people who have similar interests, it can make use of that data and the other user’s watch history and their click through rate for a given thumbnail to recommend it for a new user who hasn’t seen the particular movie/thumbnail yet.

Since Netflix has data on clicking behavior of other people with similar interests, it is reasonable to guess that if other people with similar interests and watch history had a high click through rate on a certain thumbnail, then it is likely that this image thumbnail will perform well on a new person who hasn’t yet been recommended this movie / thumbnail.

  • It is possible that the recommended thumbnails would all appear similar and less appealing if they were optimized to look the same. Considering each thumbnail along with the other presentation will be key.
  • It should take into account what thumbnails the user previously viewed in association with this video and provide a seamless, cohesive experience with this movie.

HOW DOES NETFLIX TARGET THESE CUSTOM-CREATED THUMBNAILS TO THE APPROPRIATE INDIVIDUAL?

  • Number of movies previously watched by the user.
  • Number of minutes of each show watched.
  • Percentage of completion for every video/series watched so far.
  • Number of upvotes, which movies were favorited, etc.
  • Any seasonal or weekly trends related to a user’s level of engagement, etc.

HOW DOES THIS GROUPING OF SIMILAR USER PROFILES WORK AND HOW DOES A PRODUCT MANAGER MAKE SENSE OF THE DATA?

You see, they use this grouping of profiles to create a 360-degree profile of every user and mathematically rank each one based on hundreds, perhaps thousands of different attributes. Using such data, they are able to predict the likely behavior of other users based on the behavior of one user.

In this grouping mechanism, each Netflix user’s profile could be described through numerical values depicting their interest in certain aspects — such as the movie genre, favorite stars, or the movie topic considering each as an individual dimension.

By using these relations and groupings, the appearance of related interest groups would indicate a closer relationship, so it becomes more easy and effective to recommend shows more likely to be seen by the user.
